Can cream-skimming encourage prevention?

Master thesis

Hana Marie Smrcková

Outline

Reserch question

Motivation

Motivation

  • <1-> Physical inactivity is one of the major sources of health related problems in the developed countries.

  • <2-> Health insurance policy is likely to affect individual’s incentives to be physically active.

  • <3-> Two possible explanations with different implications for the design of health insurance system:

    • <4-> Moral hazard

      • <4-> Regulation of the premium (Community rating) on the health insurance market decreases physical activity

    • <5-> Bounded willpower

      • <5-> Regulation of the premium that motivates insurers to practice cream-skimming potentially increases physical activity

RQ

Research Question Does community rating with cream-skimming induce more physical activity than a risk-rating health insurance setting?

Theoretical framework

Insured’s decision about the level of prevention

Privately optimal level of prevention

  • Risk rating contract
    c′(p)=δ(ΔH + Δγ)

  • Community rating contract
    c′(p)=δ(ΔH)

  • Cream-skimming contract
    $$c'(p)=\delta\frac{(\Delta{H})}{(1-b)}$$

Experimental design

Basic information

Health insurance game

  • Subjects are divided into groups that imitate the incentives under the various insurance contracts

    • RR treatment

    • CRCS treatment

  • Activity is tracked by fitness wristbands

  • The experiment lasts for three months

Hypothesis

Hypothesis

  • Hypothesis 0: Subjects in the RR treatment experienced a higher level of physical activity than subjects in CRCS during the monitored period.

  • Hypothesis 1: Subjects in the RR treatment experienced a lower level of physical activity than subjects in CRCS during the monitored period.
